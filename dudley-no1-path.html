DUDLEY NO.1 PATH

FACTS AND HISTORY ABOUT DUDLEY CANAL N0.1 PATH

Dudley Canal No.1 Path is roughly 4.8 miles long, with 12 locks in between this walk. This path joins the Stourbridge Canal at the Delph Locks in Brierley Hill and continues to the "Capital Town" of the Black Country, Dudley! The Dudley Canalâ€™s original Act was passed in 1776, on the same day as that of the Stourbridge Canal, and it opened in 1779. Lord Dudley had started a cut from the Birmingham Canal at Tipton into his coal and limestone mines back in 1775.

Dudley Tunnel is a big attraction found on this path that leads all the way to the Black Country Museum!. The tunnel itself does not allow boat engines so boaters have to "leg it" through the dark tunnel... Without startling the BATS that live there!
